After class: Keep working on your projects!

Mini-project tips:

  • Panicking? There’s a good chance you’re overthinking things.
    • Figure out what the bare minimum is that you would need to do to get the points you need for a project, and just do that.
    • Still have time for a little more? Great, put in a little more to count toward you engagement score.
    • Didn’t get everything done? Kind of a bummer, but whatever. Turn in what you have!
  • Realizing you were overambitious?
    • Cut your losses and swap to an easier and/or more structured project. No judgment.
      • Actually some judgment, but in a good way. I will judge you positively for making a reasoned decision about priorities and organization.
  • Review the graded assessment files for past MPs.
    • In some cases the TAs may have indicated you can revise and resubmit, in which case you should do that!
      • If they didn’t note it on your assessment, you can ask your TA if they will accept a resubmission, but it is entirely at their discretion. Remember the general policy is no MP resubmissions allowed — this is a kindness from the TAs!
    • Note where and why you missed attempted objectives. In many cases it is for very tiny things that are very easily fixable in the next project you complete.

Final project tips:

  • Read the research-assessment.md document closely before you go to complete it for submission!!
    • Includes a thorough description of the overall expectations. Failing to meeting these will be reflected in your engagement score.
    • Includes detailed explanations for meeting each objective. Read these closely! There are many that are very easy to meet and very easy to miss
  • I may give credit for work that does not meet all the outlined expectations for an objective if I believe it has demonstrated mastery of the objective in another way
  • You are required to complete the AI statement. Failure to do so will result in a 5 point penalty.
  • Use the most recent version of the assessment document! If you don’t see a detailed list of overall expectations, a section for your AI statement, a reflection section, or a grading section, you’ve got the wrong version!
    • Aside from just missing these sections, you’re missing important clarifications and simplifications for objective expectations.
